Our Mission

Unite. Educate. Develop. Support.

Unite

Connecting coaches across every region of the commonwealth — from Appalachia to the Northern Virginia suburbs — into one statewide professional community.

Educate

Clinics, conventions, Zoom calls, and peer learning that keep you current, competitive, and sharp. The game evolves. Your coaching should too.

Develop

Development doesn't stop when your playing days end. VBCA exists to help coaches grow their craft, their careers, and their programs.

Support

Resources, recognition, and a network of coaches who understand exactly what this job demands — because they live it too.

The Reality of Coaching in Virginia

Coaching Is a Calling.
It Shouldn't Feel Like Isolation.

You give everything to your program — your players, your community, your craft. But most days, you're figuring things out alone. No statewide network. No professional community. No recognized home for Virginia baseball coaches.

The feeling isn't failure. It's the result of a gap that's existed in Virginia baseball for too long: coaches who dedicate their careers to the game but have no collective voice, no peer learning, and no institution that fights for their recognition.

Virginia has hundreds of high school and collegiate programs. Until 2018, coaches had nowhere to gather statewide.

Virginia baseball coaches have earned a professional organization that treats coaching as the serious profession it is — not just a volunteer role or a seasonal obligation.

The VBCA Difference

From Isolated to Indispensable.

Imagine showing up to the Annual Convention at UVA Darden — a room full of coaches who have faced the same challenges, built the same kinds of programs, and understand what it means to give this game everything you have. Coaches you've heard of. Coaches who will know your name.

That's what membership in the Virginia Baseball Coaches Association looks like in practice. Not a newsletter or a login portal — a community. A professional home for every coach in the commonwealth who believes that Virginia baseball should be as strong as the coaches who build it.
