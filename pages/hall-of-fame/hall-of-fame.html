VBCA Hall of Fame

The Virginia Baseball Coaches Association Hall of Fame.

Honoring the coaches who made Virginia baseball what it is.

The Honor

Virginia Baseball's Highest Coaching Recognition.

The VBCA Hall of Fame was established to preserve the legacy of coaches whose careers defined Virginia baseball — not just by wins, but by the players they developed and the communities they shaped. Inductees are retired coaches who made lasting contributions to the game at the youth, high school, or collegiate level across the commonwealth.

The induction ceremony is held annually at the VBCA Annual Convention evening reception — at UVA Darden School of Business, Charlottesville — and is open to the public.

Thirty-six coaches have been inducted across four classes (2022–2025). Each one represents a career's worth of impact on Virginia baseball.

Annual Awards

Presented at the Hall of Fame Ceremony.

Two awards accompany the Hall of Fame induction ceremony each year, recognizing excellence in the current coaching landscape.

Coach of the Year Award

Presented to a Virginia baseball coach whose performance and leadership during the current season exemplifies the standard the VBCA was built to promote.

Award criteria and nomination details to be announced.

Baseball Impact Award

Presented to an individual — coach, administrator, or organization — whose contributions to Virginia baseball extend beyond the field and have made a measurable difference in the game statewide.

Award criteria and nomination details to be announced.
