The Game

Virginia Baseball's Senior Showcase.

The Virginia Baseball All-Star Game is a flagship VBCA program — one of the most meaningful events in the high school coaching calendar. Senior players are nominated by their coaches, selected through regional committees, and brought together for a single showcase game that honors their careers and the programs that developed them.

For coaches, the game is a recognition of the work you put into your senior class. For players, it is the final opportunity to compete alongside the best the commonwealth has to offer.

Nominations

How Coaches Nominate Senior Players.

Nominating your players for the Virginia All-Star Game is a direct acknowledgment of what they've built over four years. The process is designed to be straightforward — because the focus should be on the players, not the paperwork.

Eligibility

  • Active senior players in good academic and athletic standing
  • Nominated by their head or assistant coach
  • Eligible regardless of college commitment status

The Process

  1. 1 Review eligibility criteria for the 2026 game
  2. 2 Submit your nomination using the form below
  3. 3 Regional committee reviews nominations and confirms the roster
